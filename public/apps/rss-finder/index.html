RSS Crawler

Discover, inspect, and export RSS and Atom feed URLs on Windows.

windows
overview
RSS Crawler finds RSS and Atom feeds from websites, including endpoints that are not prominently linked on the page. It helps you review discovered feed URLs and export organized feed lists for research, monitoring, newsletter, and automation workflows. A Windows crawler can be faster than manually inspecting source, headers, and common feed paths across multiple sites.
workflow
  1. Paste a website URL.
  2. Discover RSS, Atom, and likely feed endpoints.
  3. Review, test, and export feed URLs.
use cases
  • find RSS feeds from websites
  • discover hidden RSS or Atom feed URLs
  • build feed lists for monitoring
  • collect sources for newsletters, research, or automation
who for
For researchers, automation users, newsletter builders, bloggers, OSINT workflows, and anyone building a list of source feeds.
faq
Does RSS Crawler require a BassThermal account?
No. RSS Crawler does not require a BassThermal account.
What does it find?
It is meant to find RSS and Atom feed URLs from websites and help inspect/export discovered feeds.
